## FAQ

**Q: What does GaN mean?**
A: GaN stands for Gallium Nitride, a semiconductor material that allows for more efficient power delivery in smaller chargers compared to traditional silicon-based chargers.

**Q: How many watts do I need to charge my MacBook Pro?**
A:  Most modern MacBook Pros benefit from at least 60W of power delivery. The 16” models often require 96W or higher for optimal charging speeds, and the latest M3 Max versions can draw up to 140W.

**Q: Are GaN chargers safe?**
A: Yes, reputable GaN chargers undergo rigorous safety testing and certifications to ensure they are safe to use with your devices. Look for chargers with UKCA marking and relevant safety standards compliance.
